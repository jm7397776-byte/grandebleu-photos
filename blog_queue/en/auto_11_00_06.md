# Embracing Jeju's Gentle Embrace: The Best Season for Your First Yacht Journey

My heart always stirs when planning a trip to Jeju. This island, a canvas of ever-changing beauty, promises something new with each visit. For first-time visitors, especially, deciding when to truly experience its magic can be a delightful dilemma. After experiencing the serene waters with Grande Bleu Yacht, I can confidently share that early summer – specifically June – offers an exquisite balance, painting the perfect backdrop for your inaugural yacht journey.

## Why June Beckons: A Symphony of Calm and Clarity

Imagine the gentle sea breeze, the sun's warm caress without the intense summer heat, and skies often clear enough to see forever. June in Jeju embodies this ideal. The waters around Daepo Port, uniquely calm and sheltered, become an inviting stage for a catamaran sailing yacht. This season allows for an unparalleled appreciation of the coastal marvels without the extremes of other months. It's truly a time when the island whispers its most welcoming secrets.

## Your Voyage Aboard Grande Bleu: A Seamless Experience

Our journey began at Daepo Port in Seogwipo. The Grande Bleu Yacht, a magnificent catamaran sailing yacht, awaited with its impressive capacity – Yacht 550 seats 44, and Yacht 620 seats 47 – ensuring ample space for comfort. The promise of a licensed captain and engineer onboard immediately instilled a sense of ease, a comforting thought for any first-time voyager. It’s a point of pride that Grande Bleu is certified as the only brand in Korea where guests can embark without a life vest, a testament to its unparalleled safety standards in the calmest waters of Jeju.

## Coastal Wonders and Culinary Delights

For an hour, we gracefully glided along the Daepo coastline. The sight of Wolpyeong Jusangjeolli, with its towering hexagonal columns, felt otherworldly. Passing by Wolpyeong Sea Cave and the majestic Elephant Rock, each landmark unveiled a new facet of Jeju's geological artistry. This season enhanced the clarity, allowing for breathtaking views.

Adding to the experience was the array of complimentary treats: refreshing draft beer, fine wine, sweet Jeju tangerine juice, bottled water, snacks, and even cup ramen. It transforms a scenic tour into a floating lounge, a true '선상푸드코트' (onboard food court) that elevates the simple joy of being at sea. I also spotted others trying to fish for rockfish, scorpionfish, filefish, and pufferfish, adding an element of playful adventure.
