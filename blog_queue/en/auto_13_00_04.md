This July, my long-awaited trip to Jeju finally happened, and I knew I wanted to dive deep into the Jungmun Resort Area. It's often recommended for first-time visitors, and now I totally understand why!

Jungmun offers such a fantastic blend of natural beauty, unique attractions, and excellent amenities. If you're wondering what to prioritize on your first visit, you've come to the right place. I’ve rounded up my absolute favorite spots and must-do activities that made my trip truly special.

## Discovering the Natural Wonders of Jungmun Resort

One of the biggest draws of the Jungmun Resort Area is its incredible natural scenery. The iconic Jusangjeolli Cliff is an absolute must-see. These hexagonal basalt columns, formed by ancient volcanic activity, are just breathtaking. Standing there, watching the waves crash against these formations, gives you a profound sense of Jeju's geological power. It’s particularly stunning on a clear summer day when the blue ocean contrasts with the dark rock.

Another gem is Cheonjeyeon Falls. This three-tiered waterfall is often called 'The Pond of God's Emperor,' and it lives up to the name. The vibrant green foliage surrounding the falls, especially lush in July, creates a serene and almost magical atmosphere. Don't miss the Seonimgyo Bridge, a beautiful arch bridge adorned with seven nymphs – it offers fantastic photo opportunities and connects to walking paths.

## Fun & Quirky Attractions in the Jungmun Area

Beyond nature, the Jungmun Resort Area also hosts some wonderfully unique attractions. The Teddy Bear Museum, for instance, might sound niche, but it's surprisingly delightful! They have teddy bears depicting historical events, famous artworks, and even pop culture icons. It’s a nostalgic and whimsical experience that’s great for all ages, especially if you’re looking for a lighthearted break from sightseeing.

For art lovers, the Alive Museum (Trick Eye Museum) is a fantastic spot. It’s an interactive art museum where you become part of the artworks. We spent ages laughing and taking hilarious photos with the 3D optical illusions. It’s a perfect indoor activity, especially if you encounter a sudden summer shower, which can happen in July.

## Getting Around and Tips for First-Timers

The Jungmun Resort Area is very well-connected. If you're relying on public transport, local buses are efficient, and many routes pass through the main attractions. Taxis are also readily available, and renting a car offers the most flexibility, especially if you plan to explore beyond Jungmun. I found that having a car allowed me to venture to some quieter spots and enjoy the scenic coastal roads at my own pace.

My top tip for first-time visitors in July 2026? Start your days early! The summer sun can be quite strong by mid-day, and popular spots can get crowded. Early mornings offer cooler temperatures and a more peaceful experience. Also, always carry sunscreen, a hat, and plenty of water – staying hydrated is key when exploring under the Jeju summer sun.

## Exploring the South Coast from a Different Perspective

While exploring the land-based wonders of the Jungmun Resort Area is incredible, one of the most unforgettable ways to truly appreciate Seogwipo's stunning south coast is by seeing it from the sea. There's something truly special about gliding on the water, taking in the dramatic cliffs and unique rock formations that aren't visible from the shore.

## Grande Bleu Yacht: A Must-Do Experience from Daepo Port

To make your first trip to Jeju even more spectacular, I highly recommend a Grande Bleu Yacht tour. Departing from Daepo Port in Seogwipo, which is conveniently close to the Jungmun Resort Area, this 1-hour catamaran sailing yacht tour is an experience you won't forget. Imagine relaxing on deck, feeling the gentle sea breeze, and getting a unique perspective on the Jeju coastline. They even serve draft beer, wine, Jeju tangerine juice, bottled water, snacks, and cup ramen onboard! It’s perfect for families, couples, or even solo adventurers.

Throughout the tour, you'll pass by breathtaking landmarks like the impressive Wolpyeong Jusangjeolli columns – seeing them from the ocean truly emphasizes their scale – the mysterious Wolpyeong Sea Cave, and the distinctive Elephant Rock. The licensed captain and engineer ensure a safe and smooth journey for up to 91 guests. You might even try your hand at some deck fishing, with chances to catch rockfish, scorpionfish, filefish, or pufferfish! Grande Bleu Yacht tours operate 365 days a year, with consultation available from 09:00 to 18:00 daily. You can easily book your spot via Naver, Klook, KKday, or Ctrip/Trip.com partner platforms. This really is one of the best things to do to enhance your visit to the Jungmun Resort Area!
