## What Makes Grande Bleu Different

Most Jeju "yacht tours" are single-hull motorboats that bounce around the moment the wind picks up. Grande Bleu runs twin-hull catamarans — the same boat design used by Caribbean charter fleets — which absorbs roughly 90% of wave force. At an average 37cm swell, you can stand at the bar holding a glass of wine without spilling it.

That's the practical difference. It's also the reason it's the only catamaran brand certified for commercial sail in Korea, and why Shilla Hotel and Parnas Jeju route their guests here.

For a 40-something traveller who's done Bali speedboats and Greek island ferries, the comparison that lands closest is a small Mediterranean day-sail — minus the cattle-class crowds.

## The Route: 60 Minutes Along Seogwipo's South Coast

The course covers six coastal landmarks on Jeju's volcanic south shore, all part of the broader UNESCO Global Geopark zone:

- **Wolpyeong Basalt Columns** — hexagonal lava cliffs meeting the sea
- **Wolpyeong Sea Cave** — narrow entrance, noticeably cooler air inside
- **Elephant Rock** — yes, it really looks like an elephant
- **Pangpaengideok** — a wide flat reef platform, oddly photogenic
- **Large Sea Cave** — the biggest on this coastline
- **Jingotnae Sea Canyon** — vertical cliff walls dropping into the water

A crew member narrates in Korean and English along the way. Nothing performative — just enough geology to know what you're looking at.

## Jeju in October: Why Autumn Is the Sweet Spot

October and November are arguably the best months for a Jeju sunset cruise. Summer humidity is gone, typhoon season has passed, and the sea settles into long stretches of glass-flat afternoons. Air temperature sits around 18–22°C — light jacket weather on deck, no shivering.

Autumn light in Jeju also runs gold for longer. The 360-degree golden hour over the basalt coast is a once-a-day window, and in autumn it lines up neatly with the late-afternoon sailing slot.

If you're a repeat visitor who's already done Hallasan and Manjanggul, this is the slow side of Jeju that doesn't make it onto K-drama location lists.

## Morning or Sunset Sail — Which to Pick?

Honest answer: both work, for different reasons.

- **Morning sail**: clearer light, sharper photos, calmer sea, fewer crowds
- **Sunset sail**: golden hour, deeper sea colour, the signature shot

If it's your first time, take the sunset. If it's a repeat visit, morning is the underrated pick.
