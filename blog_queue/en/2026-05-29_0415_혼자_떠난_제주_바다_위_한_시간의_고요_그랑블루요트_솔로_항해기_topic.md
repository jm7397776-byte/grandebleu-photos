## Why a Catamaran Beats a Single-Hull (Especially in June–August)

Jeju summers (Jun–Aug) bring warm air, calm mornings and the kind of golden-hour light that flatters every photo. They also bring swell. The reason **Grande Bleu Jeju** is worth flagging over a standard sightseeing boat is the twin-hull design — two parallel hulls cutting the water instead of one, which means significantly less roll.

If you've ever sworn off boat trips because of seasickness, this is the design that changes your mind. It's also the only catamaran in Korea carrying full brand certification, which matters more than it sounds: a licensed captain and engineer are onboard every sailing, not just one or the other.

For couples doing **Jeju sailing** for the first time, this is the practical detail that separates a relaxing afternoon from a regrettable one.

## The Route: Wolpyeong Cliffs, Sea Caves and Elephant Rock

Departing Daepo Port, the boat tracks the southern Seogwipo coastline at a slow, photo-friendly pace. The highlight reel includes:

- **Elephant Rock (Kkokkiribawi)** — a cliff face that genuinely does look like an elephant drinking
- **Jingotnae Sea Canyon** — vertical walls dropping straight into the water
- **Wolpyeong Basalt Columns** — hexagonal volcanic columns, part of Jeju's UNESCO Geopark geology
- **Wolpyeong Sea Cave** and the **Large Sea Cave (Keunbada Donggul)** — best viewed in silence so you can hear the swell echo
- **Pangpaengideok** — a wide flat reef plateau that photographs better than it has any right to

Seeing the basalt columns from sea level rather than the standard tourist viewpoint above is the bit most guidebooks miss. It's the same UNESCO geology — different angle, far fewer people in your shot.

## Fishing: Built In, Not an Add-On

Every tour includes a short fishing session. Rods and bait are provided, and the crew talks beginners through it in about two minutes. The common catches in this stretch of water are rockfish, scorpionfish, filefish and the occasional puffer (which gets a polite release).

It's not about the catch. It's the ten quiet minutes where nobody's checking their phone — which, if you came to Jeju for **solo healing** or a slower pair of days with your partner, is exactly the point.

## Practical Info for International Travellers

- **Getting there:** ~50 minutes by car from Jeju International Airport. Without a rental, take the intercity bus to Jungmun and a short taxi to Daepo Port.
- **Best season:** June–August for warm weather and longest daylight; sea is generally calmest in the morning.
- **What to bring:** Sunglasses, light jacket (sea breeze even in summer), seasickness tablets if you're sensitive — pharmacies near Daepo Port are limited.
- **5-star credentials:** Official partnerships with Shilla Hotel and Parnas Jeju.
