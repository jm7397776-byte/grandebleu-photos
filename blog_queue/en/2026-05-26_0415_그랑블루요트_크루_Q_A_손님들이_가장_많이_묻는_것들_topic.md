## Q2. How big is the boat, and how many people on board?

Total capacity is 91 guests across two catamarans — Boat 550 holds 44, Boat 620 holds 47.

A catamaran has two hulls instead of one, so it rocks far less than a standard monohull yacht. The deck is wide, you can move around freely, and families with kids aren't tripping over each other.

Groups often ask if there's room for everyone together. Weekday departures usually have space; weekends and **Jeju October** peak slots fill earlier, so book ahead.

## Q3. What will I actually see on the route?

The cruise runs about one hour. That sounds short until you realise it covers the headline geology of Seogwipo's south coast.

Three landmarks anchor the route:

- **Elephant Rock** — a sea stack that genuinely looks the part
- **Wolpyeong Sea Cave** — carved into the volcanic cliff
- **Wolpyeong Jusangjeolli** — hexagonal basalt columns

Most visitors only see the Jusangjeolli columns from the clifftop viewing platform. From sea level you read the height and texture differently — same rock, different story. It's the kind of comparison Greek-islands sailing can't offer because the geology simply isn't there.

## Q4. What's the food and drink situation?

Included in the ticket:

- Draft beer
- Bottled water
- Snacks
- Jeju sweet-shrimp cup ramen (you won't find this one elsewhere)

If you fancy a go at fishing, filefish, scorpionfish and pufferfish come up regularly. The crew handles the catch — pufferfish in particular you don't touch yourself, for obvious reasons.

It's not a tasting menu. It's a one-hour cruise with enough to keep you happily fed and watered, which is exactly what most people want.

## Q5. Who's in charge of safety?

A licensed captain and a licensed engineer are on every departure. One watches the weather and sea conditions, the other monitors the engines.

Here's the bit guests are usually surprised by: **Grande Bleu is the only yacht in Jeju where life jackets aren't compulsory on boarding.** That clearance comes from the catamaran hull's stability rating and the deck design passing safety inspection.

Jackets are stowed onboard and you can wear one any time you like. Most guests don't — partly comfort, partly because photos and reels look better without the orange foam. Your call.

## Q6. Luxury vs Sunset — what's the difference?

The route and duration are identical. What changes is the light.

- **Luxury (daytime)** — sharp colours, clear water, best for photos and family trips with kids
- **Sunset** — departs late afternoon, returns into the **Jeju autumn sunset**

September to November is genuinely the sweet spot for the sunset run. The humidity drops, visibility sharpens, and the light goes gold rather than hazy. Plenty of guests do both runs across two days — same coastline, completely different mood.
