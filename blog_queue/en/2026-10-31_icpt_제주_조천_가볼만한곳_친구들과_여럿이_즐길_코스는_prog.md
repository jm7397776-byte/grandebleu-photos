### A Slice of Paradise: Coastal Highlights & Dolphin Spotting

Setting sail from Daepo Port in Seogwipo, your **Jeju sailing** adventure immediately immerses you in the island's breathtaking coastal scenery. The 60-minute route unveils a series of natural wonders, each more impressive than the last. These geological formations, part of **Jeju**'s unique volcanic landscape, offer incredible photo opportunities and a deeper appreciation for **Korea**'s natural heritage.

*   **Elephant Rock (Kkokkiribawi):** A remarkable natural cliff shaped uncannily like an elephant, reaching its trunk into the sea.
*   **Jingotnae Sea Canyon:** Witness dramatic vertical walls forming a deep sea canyon, carved by centuries of ocean waves.
*   **Wolpyeong Basalt Columns (Wolpyeong Jusangjeolli):** These hexagonal volcanic sea cliffs are reminiscent of the Giant's Causeway, showcasing **Jeju**'s UNESCO-style geological marvels.
*   **Wolpyeong Sea Cave & Large Sea Cave (Keunbada Donggul):** Mysterious sea caves where the ocean crashes, adding a touch of drama to the serene cruise.
*   **Pangpaengideok:** A wide, flat reef plateau, perfect for experiencing the raw power of the ocean at sea level.

One of the most anticipated moments for many guests is the chance encounter with wild Indo-Pacific bottlenose dolphins. While sightings aren't guaranteed (they are wild, after all!), the Grande Bleu sails through known dolphin habitats, increasing your chances of witnessing these magnificent creatures playfully swimming alongside the yacht. Imagine the excitement for your **Jeju friends trip** group when a pod of dolphins suddenly appears – it’s a moment that truly makes the **Jeju yacht tour** unforgettable.
