# Jeju Yacht Tour — The Catamaran | Grande Bleu Catamaran, Seogwipo Jeju (first-time visitors)

## Today's Angle
This post focuses on **The Catamaran**. Grande Bleu sails from Daepo Port, Seogwipo, Jeju on a catamaran sailing yacht, with a cruise time of about 1 hour.

It is written for first-time visitors, with practical details that help before booking.

## What You See On The Route
The route introduces Jeju's southern coastline from sea level: Wolpyeong Jusangjeolli, Wolpyeong Sea Cave, Elephant Rock, and the Daepo coastline. The basalt columns feel different when viewed from the water, because you see the height, texture, and coastline in one frame.

This is not a half-day tour. It is a compact one-hour sailing experience that fits easily into a Jeju itinerary.

## Ship And Safety
Grande Bleu operates catamarans, not single-hull boats. Capacity is 91 guests total: Yacht 550 seats 44 and Yacht 620 seats 47. Each sailing includes a licensed captain and engineer onboard.

For families, older travelers, and anyone worried about motion, the twin-hull structure is one of the clearest reasons to choose this style of boat.

## Onboard Inclusions
Onboard items include draft beer, wine, Jeju tangerine juice, bottled water, snacks, and cup ramen. The experience is not a formal restaurant cruise; it is a relaxed sea-view hour with drinks, light food, photos, and coastal scenery.

For photos, watch three moments: just after departure, when the yacht approaches the coastline, and when the light changes before returning to port.
